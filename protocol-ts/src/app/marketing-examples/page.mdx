## White Backgrounds (Yellow as Accent Only) {{ id: 'white-backgrounds' }}

On white backgrounds, yellow is used sparingly as an accent. Black dominates for readability.

      <span className="rounded bg-[#FFCB05] px-2 py-1 text-zinc-900">AND</span>{' '}
      the body you deserve
    </h2>
    <div className="mt-6 text-lg text-zinc-700 dark:text-zinc-300">
      Most business programs ignore your health. Most fitness programs ignore your business. We integrate both because you can't sustain success in one without the other.
    </div>
    <div className="mt-8 flex flex-wrap gap-4">
      <button className="rounded-lg bg-zinc-900 px-6 py-3 font-semibold text-white dark:bg-white dark:text-zinc-900">

      </button>
      <button className="rounded-lg bg-[#FFCB05] px-6 py-3 font-semibold text-zinc-900">
        Get Started
      </button>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

### Best Practices for White Backgrounds

- **Black for body text**: Maximum readability (21:1 contrast)
- **Yellow for accents**: Highlights, badges, important CTAs
- **Never yellow body text**: Fails WCAG contrast requirements
- **Use yellow sparingly**: Strategic emphasis only

## All Caps Typography {{ id: 'all-caps-typography' }}

ALL CAPS with tight line height creates powerful, commanding headlines.

    <h1 className="mt-2 text-6xl font-black leading-none tracking-tight text-white">

    </h1>
  </div>
</div>

<Note>
  **Use ALL CAPS sparingly** - Great for hero sections and impactful statements, but use sentence case for body text and longer content for better readability.
</Note>
