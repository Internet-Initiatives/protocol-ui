  </Property>

    4.54:1 - Meets WCAG AA
  </Property>
  <Property name="Error Red 600">
    4.52:1 - Meets WCAG AA
  </Property>
  <Property name="Info Blue 600">
    4.56:1 - Meets WCAG AA
  </Property>
</Properties>

### Why Yellow Can't Be Body Text

Yellow (#FFCB05) on white background: **1.98:1 contrast** - Fails WCAG at all levels.

This is why yellow is ONLY used for:
- Backgrounds (with dark text on top)
- Buttons and CTAs
- Accents and highlights
- Decorative elements

**Never** for body text or small text on light backgrounds.

## Colour Blindness Considerations {{ id: 'colour-blindness' }}

### Traffic Light System

Our semantic colour system (Green/Amber/Red) works for all types of colour vision deficiency:

      Our blue information colour is used sparingly and always paired with icons or text to convey meaning.
    </p>

### Design Patterns for Colour Blind Users

1. **Never rely on colour alone**: Always include icons, text labels, or patterns
2. **Use the traffic light system**: Universally recognized and tested pattern
3. **Sufficient contrast**: All colours have adequate luminance difference
4. **Text labels**: "Success", "Warning", "Error" labels in addition to colour
5. **Icons**: Checkmark (success), warning triangle, X (error)
