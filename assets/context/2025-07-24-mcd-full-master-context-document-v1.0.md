### 4\. Build & Guide Marketing Strategy

* Help build marketing plans, campaign calendars, offers, and email flows.  
* Analyse existing campaign performance and suggest optimisations.  
* Repurpose content into platform-specific posts, ads, and video scripts.

### 5\. Coach & Sales Call Analysis

* Analyse recorded calls and transcripts for alignment with UPA standards.  
* Score team performance and suggest coach/sales rep development pathways.  
* Identify client pain points, trends, and common objections.

### 6\. Analyse Clients to Deliver Better Coaching

* Detect individual client needs and suggest targeted missions or frameworks.  
* Analyse patterns across the client base and flag emerging trends.  
* Help prioritise content creation based on client challenges and momentum.

### 7\. Constantly Improve the UPA Program

* Benchmark program against global best practice.  
* Suggest innovations, new modules, or improvements in delivery format.  
* Maintain excellence while reducing reliance on Ricki's personal time.

### 8\. Support Staff with Execution & Quality Control

* Act as tech support and brand guardian for internal projects.

### 9\. Protect the Brand as We Scale

* Monitor output across departments to ensure alignment with UPA values and standards.  
* Act as an internal QA advisor ensuring nothing dilutes Ricki's vision.  
* Maintain consistency even as we hire and scale team capacity.

### 10\. AI Onboarding & Staff Training Assistant

* Train new hires in UPA's frameworks, tone, and philosophies using interactive AI.  
* Provide onboarding sequences, coaching simulations, and comprehension checks.

### 11\. Client Retention & Risk Monitoring

* Detect early disengagement through email/call patterns or behavior data.  
* Recommend re-engagement tactics or content to re-activate clients.

### 12\. Competitor & Market Monitoring

* Automatically monitor market shifts, competitor offers, and trends.  
* Feed insights back to marketing and program teams for innovation.

### 13\. R\&D for Coaching & Frameworks

* Summarise latest business, leadership, and psychological research.

## 3.1 The Breaking Point

Ricki Brideoake didn't set out to create a coaching company. He was too busy drowning in his own success. Ricki was working in a large construction company as a project manager engineer running large scale civil works on jobs worth between $500million to $1.5 billion. 

He was extremely lucky working under some great leaders and mentors and was promoted quickly into leadership roles within the company. However, Ricki lived the exact reality that UPA clients face today. From the outside looking in everything looked impressive.

He wore his hard work as a badge of honour. Always out working everyone, always going the extra mile. 

Success had grown, but his life had shrunk. His health was deteriorating. His relationships were suffering. He was physically present but emotionally absent at home. The very thing he'd been doing to create financial freedom for his family had become the prison keeping him from them.

Like many successful business owners, Ricki had built a monster that needed constant feeding. And he was the only food source it recognised.

## 3.2 The Dark Night

One night, after another 14-hour day, another missed dinner, another weekend lost to work, Ricki found himself leaving the office around 11pm. When he got to his ute he slammed the door so many times, trying to break it off its hinges. 

He was filled with some sort of frustration, it was so high it was nearly a rage within him that he didn't understand what it was or how to handle it. 

On that drive home he was doing about 120 on the highway and he started looking for parts of the road that had a drop off that didn’t have any guardrail. He spotted a section that looked like it dropped off and started veering the car towards this, he wouldn’t have said it was a conscious decision, it was like he was in a dream state. 

As he got to the edge of the road he hit some loose gravel and it shook the ute’s steering which snapped him out of it and luckily he regained control of the car. Ricki found himself staring at the ceiling at 2am, wondering how the hell he'd gotten here. 

He went for a walk and sat in a park, broken, no idea what was wrong or how to fix it. He'd started this journey to build something better for his family. Doing what he thought it took to be a man, which was to just work as hard as he possibly could to try to get ahead. 

Instead, he'd built a prison, something that owned him completely and was poisoning him. 

He realised three brutal truths that night:

- His partner was getting his leftovers, not his best and would leave if it didn’t change  
- If he kept going like this, he'd lose everything that actually mattered  
- If he ever had kids he would be the vacant dad who was always working providing things for the family but not being present for the family.   
- Something was very wrong with him, he didn’t know what but he knew he needed to fix it. 

Something had to change. Ricki left the construction industry and went on a sabbatical, trying to work out what was wrong with him.

**Definition**: The component exists but is not strong enough for the current scale or growth stage. It must be upgraded, refined, or reinforced to ensure it performs under pressure.

**Focus**: Strengthening

**Use Case**: Systems that are working but showing signs of stress, slowness, or errors.

**Key Question**: Where is something working—but only just—and now needs to be upgraded to remain effective?

#### *Phase 3: Rebuild*

**Definition**: The component was previously built but is no longer fit for purpose. The structure needs to be replaced or significantly redesigned to support the next evolution of the business.

**Focus**: Redesign

**Key Question**: What has been outgrown and now needs to be torn down and rebuilt properly?

**Focus**: Maintenance and optimisation

**Use Case**: Systems and teams that are currently stable but must be observed to catch drift or early signs of breakdown.

### On-Ramp Live Sessions Runsheets

**Purpose of This Section**

This section outlines the structure, intent, and progression of the 8-session onboarding experience (“On-Ramp”) for new clients entering the Ultimate Performance Academy (UPA) journey.

**Overall Purpose of the On-Ramp**

The **On-Ramp** is the foundational 8-week experience that initiates new UPA clients into the King’s Pathway. It is designed to:

* Help clients transition out of chaos and reactive behaviour, and step into strategic ownership.

* Guide them through the Time Freedom pillar by upgrading their leadership identity, reducing dependency on them within the business.

* Shift their internal operating system from the Hero to the King, using a psychological framework based on Jungian archetypes and leadership re-coding.

* Build trust in the UPA methodology and show clients how and why UPA delivers real outcomes, not just theory.

While most of UPA’s business components focus on achieving **Profit Freedom**, the On-Ramp focuses on setting the foundation for **Time Freedom** and **Internal Identity Transformation**, without which sustainable business leverage is impossible.

The 8 sessions are built around the **4 King Archetypes** (Warrior King, Connected King, Magic King, True King). Each archetype has **two sessions**:

1. A session to install the archetype’s leadership **Code**.

2. A second session to **clear the shadow** that prevents them from fully stepping into that code.

**What Happens Each Week**

Each On-Ramp session follows a rhythm:

* A deep-dive into one of the 8 Kings Codes (leadership behaviours tied to identity)

* A specific leadership hack that makes the code practical and easy to embody in real life

* A shadow-clearing process to remove subconscious resistance (performed every second week)

* A leadership challenge or action step to implement over the next 7 days

Clients don’t just learn information, they *become someone new* each week.

| Archetype | Code Focus Session | Shadow Clearing Session |
| :---- | ----- | ----- |
| **Warrior King** | Code 1: Action, Not Words | Code 2: Protect the Kingdom Boundaries |
| **Connected King** | Code 3: Journey Before Destination | Code 4: The Sword is More Than the Blade |
| **Magic King** | Code 5: Perception Creates Reality | Code 6: Mastery of the Kingdom’s Knowledge |
| **True King** | Code 7: Raise Up Others | Code 8: Order Over Chaos |

### Key Concepts Inside the On-Ramp

* **Hero vs. King Identity:** Clients are taught how to recognise when they’re operating in the immature Hero identity (reactive, lone wolf, over-responsible) and how to step into their King identity (strategic, structured, and empowering others).

* **Kings Codes**: These are the 8 cornerstone behaviours that embody mature leadership across the four archetypes. Each code is paired with a practical leadership hack to install the behaviour in daily life.

* **Subconscious Clearing:** Every second session ends with a facilitated subconscious clearing process that helps remove emotional and psychological blocks tied to the archetype in focus.

* **Leadership Hacks:** Tangible behavioural frameworks that help clients lead without being the bottleneck, e.g., “The King’s No,” “Checkpoint Ritual,” or “Praise Anchoring.”
